A Home in Music city

In 1903, on the corner of 17th and McGavock, the Addison Ave. Cumberland Presbyterian Church built a beautiful brick sanctuary. In 1968, Fred Foster, founder of Monument Records, purchased the church and converted it into a recording studio. In 2012, Zac Brown bought the same building and transformed it into a first-class facility that inspires creation and satisfies the appetites of artists and musicians from around the world.

Matt Mangano

Position at Southern Ground Nashville:
Studio Director

Originally from:
Visalia, CA

How you got to Nashville:
I came to Nashville to join a band. The band didn't work out, but it led to many other fantastic opportunities that made me decide to stay.

How you got to SGN:
I was working for Zac's record label, making records in my basement studio. The time came for the operation to grow, and I helped Zac find the facility and open the studio.

Favorite Studio Mama Creation:
Levi Chicken followed by pound cake. Actually, forget the chicken - I'll just have the pound cake.

Like most about SGN:
The studio is a home away from home. Every single bit of this place was made with love, especially the love for music, art, and the preservation of their history.

Best part of your job:
Working with my co-workers. Not only are they immensely talented in their craft, they are fiercely committed to doing whatever it takes to get the job done.

Fave piece of gear/equipment:
Ampex ATR-102
